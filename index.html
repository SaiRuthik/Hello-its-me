About

I'm a Robotics & Automation Engineer with hands-on experience across quantum photonics research, industrial automation, and commercial data engineering. My career has taken me through MSc research labs, production automation platforms, and retail tech stacks — the common thread being intelligent systems that replace tedious human effort with precise, repeatable automation.

My MSc dissertation at Heriot-Watt built AutoScan — a full automation system for quantum emitter detection in Silicon Carbide using coarse-fine motorised stages, photoluminescence mapping, spectroscopy, HBT measurements, and an intelligent decision-making pipeline.

In production, I've built ML pricing engines, OpenCV defect detection systems with cobot integration, Selenium RPA scrapers, Klaviyo CRM flows, Zapier automations, and SQL ETL pipelines processing hundreds of thousands of records. I believe the most powerful work happens when you understand both ends of the stack — from servo motors to cloud dashboards.

"Give me a tedious, error-prone manual process and I'll give you a system that does it better, faster, and while you sleep."

Mars Rover Navigation — BBR vs Evolutionary Robotics
Designed two intelligent controllers for a simulated Mars rover in Webots. Compared Behaviour-Based Robotics (BBR) — finite state machines, ground/distance/light sensors, beacon-responsive path-switching — against an Evolutionary Robotics (ER) approach using an MLP trained via Genetic Algorithm over 512 generations. BBR achieved consistent route completion (Route A ~40s, Route B ~49s). ER demonstrated the trade-off between interpretable reactive control and long-horizon evolutionary optimisation.
